Reflection

What I learned

Working through this operators activity really forced me to lock in on Java’s execution logic, especially since I couldn't just hardcode the results and had to actually compute them using the specific operators required. Tackling Part II was a bit of a head-scratcher because balancing bitwise, logical, and assignment operators while hitting exactly six outputs required a lot of precision. The trickiest part was definitely the "a++ + ++a" style expressions; it really tests your understanding of how variables are modified within a single statement. Even though I’m still getting used to the transition from C, seeing the "write once, run anywhere" efficiency play out through proper syntax and indentation made the whole process feel a lot more disciplined. Verifying the bytecode results through the JVM and making sure my logic actually produced the exact boolean and numeric results needed was a solid way to bridge the gap between theory and actual coding.
